About Me

Study

I studied at Trinity College Dublin to a Masters' Degree level. My degree was in Mechanical and Manufacturing Engineering. In this course I enhanced my problem solving skills and developed a strong work ethic. My main drive at this point was physics and maths but I gained some exposure to programming at this early stage, working with C++ for my computer modules.

For my Masters thesis, I was assessing the effectiveness of using a crash test software called MADYMO to help diagnose sports concussion injuries. My passion for software/IT started to grow during this time

Work

After university, I worked at global semiconductor manufacturer Infineon Technologies

My role involved being a product owner for a global RPA (Robotic Process Automation) bot which automated ~10,000 shipments weekly for the company across the globe. I also worked as a VBA developer in this role, automating tasks for internal staff throughout the company.

I made the decision to study Code Insititute's Full Stack Software Development course part time as I had developed a passion for software development. I graduated from the course with a merit grade and immediately got a developer job at Version 1

Now

I work as a full stack Java developer with Version 1 for the Department of Agriculture, Food and the Marine. I work on the 'BISS' website which farmers use to apply for payments and schemes provided by the department. The site uses a REST API architecture and integrates with several data sources and third-party services within the department. To date over 12,000 applications have been made using the system.
